About

Mission

We aim to provide a gentle introduction to coding for students who don’t want to commit to a full academic course. By the end of the semester, students will be equipped to join a formal CS class with confidence, learn more on their own, or simply use the skills they’ve gained so far. Eventually, we aim to publish our curriculum to enable others to teach the course with less overhead.

Values

  • Everyone can learn to code.
  • Collaboration serves us better than competition.
  • We welcome people with varying levels of experience. Rather than ignoring these differences, we'll utilize them to help everyone progress.

Structure

In Spring 2020, this course is being taught at Mount Holyoke by Eleanor Harris. The topics include basic Python and introductory web development. There are two sections, and students can attend one or both. Each meeting will include (1) interactive lecture and (2) application of the concepts through coding activities. For more information, see the schedule.
